## The Real Cost of Hosting a Telegram Bot Yourself

Most Telegram bot hosting tutorials recommend a cheap VPS and then walk you through a process that looks straightforward on paper but takes most developers most of a day to complete correctly.

Here is what self-hosting actually involves:

Choosing and provisioning a server. Picking a cloud provider, choosing an instance size, spinning up a Linux server, and configuring SSH access.

Setting up your runtime. Installing Python, Node.js, or whichever runtime your bot uses. Managing versions. Installing dependencies.

Configuring webhooks and HTTPS. Telegram only sends updates to HTTPS endpoints. This means buying a domain, pointing it at your server, installing Nginx or Caddy, and configuring SSL certificates. Certificates need to be renewed every 90 days.

Setting up process management. Your bot process needs to restart automatically if it crashes. This means configuring systemd, PM2, or Supervisor correctly.

Ongoing maintenance. Security patches, disk space monitoring, and occasional incidents that happen at the worst possible times.
